Outwork Yesterday — India’s Engineered Pressure Standard · Compression System 01

Born at Dusk

“When the gym empties, the work begins.” How a third-wash complaint on an Indian gym floor became a six-zone compression standard — engineered for the hour everyone else clocks out.

The Origin

DYUSK began, like most stubborn things, as an argument. The compression wear you could actually buy in India came in two flavours: imported kit priced like a small appliance, or local kit built to look technical without doing anything technical at all. Fabric that bagged out by the third wash. Flatlock seams that mapped to nothing in particular. Graduated pressure that graduated its way out of the garment the moment the sweat started.

We were the customers. We kept buying it, and it kept quitting long before the session did. Somewhere around the hundredth disappointing shirt, the complaint hardened into a question worth a year of our lives: where, exactly, does a working body need pressure — and how much, and for how long?

The name came from the clock

DYUSK is dusk with the work still left in it. Dusk is the hour the gym empties out: the office crowd has gone home, the playlist has gotten worse, and the only people left are the ones doing the unglamorous, unwatched work. That hour is our customer. The K on the end is for kaam — the Hindi word for work — because the whole brand is a wager on the people who keep going after everyone else has clocked out.

One standard, no decoration

We spent a year on one question and answered it with one system. Compression System 01 is two silhouettes — full sleeve and half — cut from a single graded pressure map of six zones, each tuned to how that part of the body genuinely loads under motion.

If a panel doesn’t have a job, it doesn’t ship.

No fake seams. No decorative mesh. No pressure that photographs well and performs badly. Two colourways became three — Onyx, Steel and Arctic — but the rule underneath them never moved.

Made in India

DYUSK is engineered in India, and we are not quiet about it. The same country the industry treats as a factory floor is, to us, the whole point. Our patterns are cut here. Our fabric is knitted here. And the finishing — the part most performance brands quietly ship overseas — is done alongside Indian artisans whose families have worked cloth for generations.

That heritage shows up in the details. The block-print motifs that edge our packaging are drawn from traditional hand-carved Indian prints, the kind pressed onto cloth by hand centuries before a single synthetic yarn existed. The DYUSK mark borrows the geometry of a temple lamp. We think a shirt engineered for a heavy pull and a four-hundred-year-old print tradition belong in the same sentence — and on the same label.

Work that gives back

A share of every drop funds sport for kids who otherwise couldn’t afford it — kit, coaching and floor time for young athletes across India. Outwork Yesterday was never only about us; it is the standard we are trying to hand down.

What’s next

Compression System 02 is already in the lab: new colourways, a bottoms programme and a recovery layer, all in wear-testing right now on real lifters, not mannequins. The list hears about it first. The homepage hears about it second.
